## Building a Marathon Training Plan

A good marathon training plan typically spans **16–20 weeks** and balances mileage building, recovery, and race-specific preparation. Here's how to structure one:

---

### 1. Assess Your Starting Point
- **Beginner:** Running 15–25 km/week consistently
- **Intermediate:** Running 40–60 km/week with some race experience
- **Advanced:** Running 70+ km/week with multiple race finishes
Your current fitness determines your starting weekly volume and goal pace.

---

### 2. Core Principles

**The 10% Rule** — Never increase your total weekly mileage by more than 10% from one week to the next. This prevents overuse injuries.

**The 80/20 Rule** — About 80% of your runs should be at an easy, conversational pace. Only 20% should be hard effort. Most beginners run too fast on easy days.

**Progressive Overload + Recovery** — Build for 3 weeks, then take a lighter recovery week before building again.

---

### 3. The Key Run Types

| Run Type | Purpose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Easy/Long Run** | Build aerobic base & endurance | 1–2x/week |
| **Long Run** | Your most important session | 1x/week |
| **Tempo Run** | Build lactate threshold | 1x/week |
| **Interval/Speed Work** | Improve VO2 max & pace | 1x/week |
| **Recovery Run** | Active recovery, flush fatigue | 1–2x/week |

---

### 4. Weekly Structure (Example — Intermediate)

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Monday | Rest or cross-train |
| Tuesday | Easy run (8–12 km) |
| Wednesday | Tempo or intervals (10–14 km) |
| Thursday | Easy run (6–10 km) |
| Friday | Rest or easy 6 km |
| Saturday | Easy run (10–14 km) |
| Sunday | **Long run** (16–35 km) |

---

### 5. The Long Run — Your Weekly Anchor
- Increase distance by **1–2 km per week**
- Peak long run: **30–35 km**, about 3 weeks before race day
- Run it **1–2 min/km slower** than goal marathon pace
- Practice your race-day nutrition & hydration during these runs

---

### 6. The Taper (Final 3 Weeks)
- **Week 3 before race:** Reduce volume ~20%
- **Week 2 before race:** Reduce volume ~40%
- **Race week:** Very light running, short strides, full rest 1–2 days before

---

### 7. Don't Neglect These

- 🥗 **Nutrition:** Carb-load in the final 2–3 days before the race
- 💧 **Hydration:** Practice drinking on the run during long runs
- 😴 **Sleep:** Your biggest performance enhancer
- 🧘 **Strength training:** 1–2x/week (glutes, hips, core) reduces injury risk
- 👟 **Shoes:** Get proper fitting and rotate between two pairs

---

### 8. Sample 18-Week Plan Arc

```
Weeks 1–3:   Base building (moderate volume)
Weeks 4:     Recovery week
Weeks 5–7:   Build phase 1
Week 8:      Recovery week
Weeks 9–12:  Build phase 2 (peak mileage)
Week 13:     Recovery week
Weeks 14–15: Race-specific workouts
Weeks 16–18: Taper → Race Day 🎉
```

---

### Popular Pre-Built Plans (for reference)
- **Hal Higdon** — Great for beginners/intermediates
- **Jack Daniels' Running Formula** — Science-based, intermediate/advanced
- **Nike Run Club App** — Guided audio coaching
- **Garmin Coach** — Adapts to your pace and schedule

The most important thing: **consistency over intensity**. Showing up for your easy runs every week matters more than any single hard workout. Good luck! 🏃
